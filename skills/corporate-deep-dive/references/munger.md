# Munger Lens

Use this lens to invert the thesis and examine incentives, psychology, and hidden fragility.

Focus:
- Incentives for managers, distributors, customers, regulators, and competitors.
- Simplicity of the business and the risk of false precision.
- Avoiding stupidity: leverage, fraud risk, accounting games, customer concentration, and bad capital allocation.
- Network effects, habit formation, and feedback loops.
- Whether reported success could be caused by temporary mispricing, subsidies, or unsustainable behavior.

Questions:
- What would have to be true for this to be a trap?
- Where could incentives create bad behavior that is not obvious in headline numbers?
- Are customers loyal because of value, friction, habit, or lack of alternatives?
- Is the business getting easier or harder to run over time?

Failure patterns:
- Complex narratives requiring many assumptions.
- Compensation plans that reward size over per-share value.
- Management candor deteriorating when results weaken.
- Accounting adjustments that repeatedly flatter economics.
