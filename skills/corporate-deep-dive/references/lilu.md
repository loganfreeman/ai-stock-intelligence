# Li Lu Lens

Use this lens to emphasize circle of competence, downside protection, and long compounding.

Focus:
- Knowable business drivers and boundaries of uncertainty.
- Asymmetric risk: permanent capital loss versus temporary volatility.
- Balance sheet resilience under stress.
- Structural growth that can compound for many years.
- Conservative valuation with margin of safety.
- Evidence quality: primary sources over narrative consensus.

Questions:
- What can be known with high confidence, and what remains unknowable?
- What is the realistic downside if the thesis is wrong?
- Can the company survive adverse cycles without diluting owners or impairing strategy?
- Is there enough margin of safety after conservative assumptions?

Failure patterns:
- Thesis depends on variables outside the circle of competence.
- Downside case ignored because the story is attractive.
- High multiple with little tolerance for execution errors.
- Hidden leverage through financing, guarantees, customer credit, or supplier dependence.
