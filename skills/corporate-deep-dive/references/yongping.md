# Yongping Lens

Use this lens to evaluate whether the company creates durable customer value.

Focus:
- Product truth: customers return because the product or service is genuinely useful.
- Pricing power used with restraint, not extraction that invites churn or regulation.
- Brand trust, user experience, and repeat behavior.
- Culture that prioritizes long-term value over short-term reported performance.
- Competitive comparison from the customer's point of view.

Questions:
- Why do customers choose this product when they have alternatives?
- Is the company making users' lives easier, cheaper, safer, or more productive?
- Could aggressive monetization damage the brand or future demand?
- Does management understand what not to do?

Failure patterns:
- Growth driven mainly by promotion, channel stuffing, or one-time demand.
- Product quality lagging the brand story.
- High margins that depend on customer confusion or switching friction alone.
- Short-term monetization that weakens trust.
