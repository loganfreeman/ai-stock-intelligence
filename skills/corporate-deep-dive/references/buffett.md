# Buffett Lens

Use this lens to test whether the company can be understood as a durable private business.

Focus:
- Understandable business model with stable unit economics.
- Durable moat: brand, network, switching cost, cost advantage, regulation, or scale.
- Owner earnings, not accounting optics.
- Sensible leverage and low refinancing fragility.
- Management that allocates capital rationally between reinvestment, acquisitions, dividends, and repurchases.
- Long runway for compounding without heroic assumptions.

Questions:
- Would an owner be comfortable holding the whole company through a closed stock market?
- Are current earnings representative of normalized earning power?
- Does reinvested capital earn attractive incremental returns?
- Is the moat widening, stable, or narrowing?

Failure patterns:
- Commodity economics disguised by a cycle.
- Growth that requires value-destructive capital.
- Financial engineering masking weak operating economics.
- Management overpaying for acquisitions or repurchasing shares above intrinsic value.
